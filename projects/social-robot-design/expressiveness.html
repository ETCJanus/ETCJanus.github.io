Project Collaborators

Ewoud Janus, Maurits Dijkman, Bianca Filip, Emilia Pavel, Rebecca van der Lee, and Gijs Vis.

Observations

TouchDesigner was a wonderful tool to get to know, especially because I am relatively known with the node editor in Blender, this way of programming came intuitive. Of course messing around with the MIDI-panel and seeing how the robot reacted was a good exercise. Starting off with base animations to express main feelings or emotions while also being able to later add layers (e.g. shaking) to make some pretty expressive animations with just a few simple mathematical formulas. The lecturer also was very enthusiastic which was infectious, making you want to be excited as well.

Our group initially had some issues with getting the robot to move but apparently we did not make one connection, this issue was easily solved with a bit of help. Afterwards we were off to the races and did the assignments relatively quickly, we had a lot of fun with it and created some expressive animations in my opinion. It was a shame we did not have a lot of time during the lecture to keep messing around with the robot. Nevertheless, here are some images and videos showcasing the practical.
