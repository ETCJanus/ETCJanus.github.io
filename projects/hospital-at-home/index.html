The Challenge

Hospital-at-Home programs improve efficiency but often burden patients with clinical tasks they aren't trained for. The current toolkit is a "loose collection of devices" causing cognitive overload and anxiety for older adults.

Our goal was to bridge the gap between clinical accuracy and home comfort, creating a system that reduces stress through intuitive design.

The Concept: "The Smart Cabinet"

We designed a wooden, furniture-like station that organizes all measurement tools (Blood Pressure, Oxygen, Thermometer, Scale) into a single, guided workflow.

User Journey

The interaction follows a "Poka-Yoke" (mistake-proofing) principle.

1. Wake Up: The cabinet glows softly to remind the patient.
2. The Ritual: The user presses "Start". The top shelf lights up. As they finish one task, the system automatically sends the data and lights up the next shelf.
3. Completion: After weighing, the cabinet signals "All Done" with a green glow. No apps to open, no numbers to write down.
